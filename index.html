Independent Systems Studio

Anthem builds systems between biology, design, and technology.

Projects develop as protocols, interfaces, manuals, codebases, and licensable frameworks for physical coordination, material intelligence, ecological systems, and adaptive tools.

I / Position

Anthem is not a content channel, general agency, or SaaS company. It is a project-building practice. Systems are developed internally, released selectively, and made available through public work, framework licences, or limited advisory engagements.

The work asks how people coordinate around places, materials, code, environments, tools, and biological constraints without reducing the result to branding, lifestyle, or subscription software.

II / Work

I

Arena

A protocol for bounded physical coordination. Arena begins with a site, vessel, property, field station, reserve, farm, studio, or operational container.

It defines membership, contribution, authority, custody, dispute resolution, renewal, exit, and the technical records needed to make those states legible.

II

Bloom

A site intelligence framework for land, ecological operations, buildings, gardens, farms, and regenerative properties.

Bloom studies how a place can hold access, maintenance, restoration, production, use, risk, and long-term responsibility through structured records, maps, protocols, and field interfaces.

III

ARCA

A material intelligence and provenance framework for products, fibres, supply chains, field use, repair, durability, ecological consequence, and production records.

ARCA makes process, material logic, and evidence of production legible without turning the work into marketing theatre.

IV

Orchard

An adaptive software and reasoning system for pruning complex projects.

Orchard treats ideas, codebases, protocols, and design systems as evolving structures shaped by constraints, selection, mutation, and removal.

V

FOAM

A field observation and ecological data framework for terrain, species, routes, water, disturbance, access, and local environmental change.

FOAM turns field records into usable design infrastructure: maps, logs, site data, protocols, and interfaces that help physical projects understand the environments they operate within.

III / Model

Public

Selected project pages, documents, diagrams, demos, and public releases. The public layer is an index of work, not a feed.

Licence

Defined rights to use a specific Anthem system in a specific context. A licence may include manuals, templates, repositories, reference interfaces, standards, and versioned updates.

Consult

Selective advisory or applied work only where the engagement advances Anthem’s direction. No general agency services. No open-ended implementation work by default.

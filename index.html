Research Lab and Studio

Anthem studies the systems now forming around technology, land, biological intelligence, cryptographic coordination, and new institutional life.

Independent research practice. Plettenberg Bay, South Africa. Publication, protocol work, field notes, repositories, and software experiments.

The work moves between essays, specifications, visual systems, operating documents, speculative institutions, and small tools.

00 / Statement

A research environment for future forms.

Anthem is a research lab and studio concerned with the practical architecture of emerging systems. It treats code, territory, governance, ecology, media, ritual, and economic coordination as connected design problems.

The work is neither only commentary nor only product development. It is a sequence of instruments: concepts, essays, protocols, experiments, tools, and records that test how new structures may be named, built, inhabited, and revised.

01 / Work

Current lines of work.

01

Arena

A protocol and specification layer for bounded physical environments. Arena begins with a site, vessel, property, or operational container, then defines explicit membership, contribution, authority, dispute resolution, renewal, and exit.

Digital coordination tools are treated as instruments for record, custody, treasury control, signatures, bonds, and outcomes. They do not replace the physical container. They make selected states of the container legible and verifiable.

02

Bloom

A land-oriented adaptation of the Arena frame. Bloom is concerned with farms, gardens, buildings, settlements, and regenerative sites where ecological feedback is not decorative but operational.

It studies how a place can hold use, stewardship, access, restoration, production, and long-term responsibility without becoming either a vague lifestyle brand or a rigid prescription.

03

ARCA

A provenance interface for products, materials, claims, and processes. ARCA treats the chain of production as part of the work itself: visible, attributable, recorded, and aesthetically maintained.

The value proposition is not only logo, surface, or marketing. The value is a process that can be shown with enough discipline that the process becomes inseparable from the identity of the maker.

04

Canalizing the Machine-Grown Codebase

An artistic and technical frame for AI-assisted software growth. The codebase is treated less as a mechanical construction site and more as an evolving organism constrained by seed, environment, pruning, and selective pressure.

The project asks how ideas should carry their own developmental grammar, how agents should encounter constraints, and how human attention can tend rather than merely command machine-generated systems.

05

Anatomy of Exit

An essay project on the nation-state as a retention machine. The state is examined as an enclosure system that bundles territory, identity, law, records, taxation, labor, capital, legitimacy, and enforcement.

Exit is treated as a structural political option: the ability of persons, capital, work, affiliation, records, legal functions, and governance functions to detach, route around, and recombine elsewhere.

06

Runlist

A minimal intention and execution interface for agentic work. A list is not treated as passive task storage. It becomes an operational surface where a human names intentions and an AI agent executes bounded actions.

The aim is a restrained tool for directed work rather than a productivity dashboard.
